Projectile Motion Study Materials

Facts, assumptions, and equations to understand projectile motion.

Assumptions and Model

  • Motion in a uniform gravitational field with constant acceleration g downward.
  • No air resistance (drag) and no wind.
  • Launch from position (x₀, y₀) = (0, y₀) with initial speed v₀ at angle θ above the horizontal.
  • Ground (landing) is at y = 0.
Real-world motion can deviate due to air resistance, spin (Magnus effect), and uneven terrain. This simulator uses the ideal model.

Vector Decomposition

The initial velocity vector v₀ splits into horizontal and vertical components:

v₀ₓ = v₀ cos θ,    v₀ᵧ = v₀ sin θ

Visualization:

Equations of Motion (No Air Resistance)

Position

x(t) = (v₀ cos θ) t
y(t) = y₀ + (v₀ sin θ) t − ½ g t²

Velocity

vₓ(t) = v₀ cos θ    (constant)
vᵧ(t) = v₀ sin θ − g t

Acceleration

aₓ(t) = 0,    aᵧ(t) = −g

Trajectory in x-y form

y(x) = y₀ + x tan θ − (g x²) / (2 v₀² cos² θ)

Key Results

Time to Peak

tpeak = (v₀ sin θ) / g

Maximum Height

ymax = y₀ + (v₀² sin² θ) / (2 g)

Time of Flight (landing at y = 0)

Solve −½ g t² + (v₀ sin θ) t + y₀ = 0
tflight = [v₀ sin θ + √(v₀² sin² θ + 2 g y₀)] / g

We take the positive root; the other root is negative and non-physical for forward time.

Range (Horizontal Distance)

R = (v₀ cos θ) · tflight
Special case y₀ = 0:   R = (v₀² sin 2θ) / g

Horizontal distance to peak

xpeak = (v₀ cos θ) · tpeak = (v₀² sin θ cos θ) / g

Speed at time t

|v(t)| = √[ (v₀ cos θ)² + (v₀ sin θ − g t)² ]

Optimal angle

For y₀ = 0 and fixed v₀, range is maximized at θ = 45°

Energy Viewpoint

K = ½ m |v|²,    U = m g y
At peak: vᵧ = 0, so K = ½ m (v₀ cos θ)²,   U = m g ymax

Units

  • Speed: m/s, Angle: degrees (θ), Position: meters (x, y), Acceleration: m/s².
  • Gravity near Earth: g ≈ 9.81 m/s².
